Nothing beats a good night's sleep. It will not only improve our brain's performance and our mood but also for our overall health. Sleep is generally considered as 'downtime' for the body and mind, but it is not entirely true.

  • While you sleep, your brain is working. For example, sleep helps prepare your brain to learn, remember, and create.
  • During sleep, the body produces growth hormones, which are necessary for muscle repair and growth.

So, a good night's sleep is must and should. Here is on how to get a good night's sleep.



Here is how aim for a good night's sleep

Maintain a regular sleep routine by going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps regulate your body's internal clock and improves the quality of your sleep.

Develop a calming pre-sleep routine to signal to your body that it's time to wind down. Activities such as reading a book, listening to soothing music, or taking a warm bath can help prepare your mind and body for sleep.

Reduce exposure to screens and bright lights at least an hour before bedtime. The blue light emitted by phones, tablets, and computers can interfere with the production of melatonin, the hormone that regulates sleep.
