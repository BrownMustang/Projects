Exercise is essential for maintaining physical and mental health. Regular physical activity strengthens muscles, improves cardiovascular health, and boosts overall energy levels. It also helps manage weight, reduces the risk of chronic diseases, and enhances mood by releasing endorphins. Incorporating exercise into daily routines promotes better sleep, reduces stress, and improves cognitive function, contributing to a higher quality of life.

Here are the minimum activity ranges to aim for good health:

Age group

Activity
Children and Adolesecents
(ages 5-17)		 A minimum of 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity a day and an aerobic activity that strengthens muscles and bones.
Adults (ages 18-64) A minimum of 150 to 300 minutes of moderate (or 75 to 150 minutes of vigorous) aerobic activity a week. Twice-weekly muscle strengthening can provide additional benefits.
Older adults (over age 65) Seniors can follow the same guidelines as younger adults but should also include strength and balance training 2 to 3 times a week to protect against falls.
Pregnant and postpartum women 150 minutes of moderate intensity aerobic activity per week including muscle-strengthening exercises.
